# Parcel Pin Landing Page Brief

## Goal

Design and build a **single-page landing page** for **Parcel Pin** that positions it as a **premium parcel-selection platform**.

This is **not** a generic real estate site, a proptech SaaS homepage, or a brochure site.

The landing page must communicate one core idea:

> **Parcel Pin helps buyers choose the right parcel with clarity.**

The experience should feel like a **top-tier agency** designed it for a **premium audience**, with strong emotional tone, quiet luxury, and clear commercial purpose.

---

## What Parcel Pin Is

Parcel Pin is a platform for **premium land selection**.

It helps buyers understand the differences between parcels that static materials fail to communicate, such as:

- orientation and light
- privacy and adjacency
- landscape and approach
- atmosphere and setting
- the emotional and practical fit of a parcel

Parcel Pin is not just about “showing parcels.”
It is about helping buyers move from:

- broad attraction
- to a confident shortlist
- to a stronger decision

---

## Positioning

### Category

Parcel Pin should be framed as:

- a **premium parcel-selection platform**
- a **decision environment**
- a more intelligent and refined way to choose land

### Not this

Do **not** position it as:

- an interactive map tool
- a 360 tour tool
- a visualization app
- a generic real estate technology product
- a dashboard product

### Core promise

Use this as the strategic foundation:

> **Parcel Pin helps buyers choose land with more confidence, context, and discernment.**

---

## Audience

### Primary audience

Affluent buyers evaluating premium land, especially in places like **José Ignacio, Uruguay**.

These buyers may be:

- second-home buyers
- lifestyle buyers
- legacy / family estate buyers
- investors with aesthetic and contextual sensitivity

### Secondary audience

Developers, project owners, and premium sales teams who want to present land at a higher standard.

## Emotional Direction

The page should feel:

- premium
- restrained
- elegant
- confident
- intelligent
- calm

### Emotional sequence

The visitor should feel, in order:

1. **Desire**
2. **Discernment**
3. **Confidence**
4. **Action**

### Tone

Use a **quiet luxury** tone.

That means:

- not flashy
- not loud
- not startup-like
- not over-written
- not poetic for the sake of sounding “luxury”

The tone should feel like:

- a premium architecture presentation
- a refined brand film translated into web
- a high-end land and place decision tool

---

## One-Page Requirement

This must be a **single page only**.

Do not create:

- multiple marketing pages
- a full site tree
- feature pages
- pricing pages
- blog pages

Only build the one-page landing page.

Use the existing route:

- `/`

Do not break existing app routes:

- `/viewer`
- `/editor`
- `/nighteditor`
- `/livingworldeditor`

The landing page should live at the root and act as the premium brand front door.

---

## Visual Direction

### Overall direction

The page should be **image-led**, **full-bleed**, and **editorial**.

Avoid:

- card-heavy layouts
- boxed SaaS hero sections
- dashboard mosaics
- unnecessary icons everywhere
- too many accent colors

### Visual references

There is a ripped Droneland reference inside the repo:

- `public/testlanding/sites/droneland.au/app/public/dl`
- `public/testlanding/sites/droneland.au/app`

Use that reference for:

- pacing
- image-led composition
- premium spacing
- floating/sticky navigation feel
- section rhythm

Do **not** copy:

- their copy
- their B2B “data platform” framing
- sports/agriculture subject matter
- industrial tone

### Color direction

Use a restrained premium palette inspired by:

- stone
- sand
- dune
- shadow
- sea light
- warm limestone
- dark mineral surfaces

Keep the palette muted and expensive.

### Typography

Typography should carry the luxury tone.

Recommended system:

- display serif for headlines
- quiet sans or mono for utility text / labels / nav

The type should feel:

- editorial
- high-contrast
- spacious
- calm

Avoid:

- generic startup sans-only treatment
- overly decorative luxury typography
- condensed or loud real estate fonts

## Structure

The landing page should have this exact narrative structure.

### 1. Hero

Purpose:

- establish the premium tone instantly
- explain that Parcel Pin is about choosing, not just browsing
- give a clear next action

Hero content:

- eyebrow: `Premium parcel selection for exceptional land`
- headline: `Choose land with clarity.`
- subhead:
  `Parcel Pin helps buyers see what static plans cannot: how orientation, privacy, context, and atmosphere shape the parcel that truly fits.`

CTAs:

- primary: `Request a private demonstration`
- secondary: `Explore the parcel experience`

Hero support blocks:

- `Place`
  `Built for premium land where atmosphere, orientation, and setting shape the final decision.`
- `Buyer`
  `Made for buyers choosing a future home, second residence, or legacy property with discernment.`
- `Outcome`
  `Move from broad attraction to a shorter, stronger shortlist of parcels that truly fit.`

### 2. Why Existing Sales Materials Fall Short

Purpose:

- explain the problem
- show why the old category is insufficient

Label:

- `Why existing sales materials fall short`

Headline:

- `Exceptional land deserves more than a brochure.`

Body:

- `Premium land is rarely decided by boundary lines alone. Static plans can show availability, but they flatten the subtle differences that determine how a place will actually feel to live in.`
- `In places like Jose Ignacio, buyers are not simply selecting a parcel. They are choosing light, exposure, privacy, approach, and the setting for a future life.`
- `Parcel Pin turns that decision from instinct alone into a more refined form of certainty.`

### 3. What Parcel Pin Reveals

Purpose:

- explain the substantive value
- show that Parcel Pin reveals the hidden differences between parcels

Label:

- `What Parcel Pin reveals`

Headline:

- `Not every parcel in an extraordinary place offers the same future.`

Intro:

- `Parcel Pin helps buyers read the differences that matter before they commit time, capital, and emotion to the wrong site.`

Subpoints:

- `Orientation and light`
  `See how sun, exposure, and atmosphere shape the lived feel of each parcel across the day.`
- `Privacy and adjacency`
  `Understand which parcels feel secluded, which feel connected, and where tradeoffs become visible.`
- `Context and setting`
  `Read the subtleties that brochures flatten: approach, landscape, surroundings, and the character of place.`
- `Selection with judgment`
  `Move beyond availability and compare parcels by lifestyle fit, future use, and emotional alignment.`

### 4. How Buyers Use It

Purpose:

- show the decision journey
- convert the platform into a buyer workflow

Label:

- `How buyers use it`

Headline:

- `From broad interest to a clear shortlist.`

Intro:

- `The experience is designed to preserve emotion while sharpening judgment. Buyers leave with fewer options and a much stronger sense of which parcel is truly right.`

Steps:

- `01 Discover`
  `Enter the landscape with context.`
  `Buyers begin by understanding the development as a living place, not as a grid of available inventory.`

- `02 Differentiate`
  `See what genuinely separates one parcel from another.`
  `The subtle distinctions in light, privacy, openness, and setting become legible enough to compare.`

- `03 Shortlist`
  `Reduce the field to the few that truly fit.`
  `The experience tightens a wide field into a smaller set of parcels with a clearer emotional and practical fit.`

- `04 Advance`
  `Move forward with stronger conviction.`
  `Developers and buyers can step into real conversations with more clarity than a brochure or static plan can provide.`

### 5. For Developers

Purpose:

- explain the B2B value without turning the page into a SaaS homepage

Label:

- `For developers`

Headline:

- `A more discerning way to present a premium development.`

Body:

- `Parcel Pin elevates project perception, but its value does not end with presentation. It helps serious buyers understand what they are choosing and why one parcel should rise above another.`

Supporting lines:

- `Present exceptional projects with the level of taste and seriousness they deserve.`
- `Help buyers understand parcel distinctions faster and with more confidence.`
- `Generate interest that is more qualified because selection replaces passive browsing.`

### 6. Final CTA Section

Purpose:

- close with emotional authority
- convert the user into an enquiry or demo request

Label:

- `Private demonstrations`

Headline:

- `Premium land should be chosen with more than instinct alone.`

Body:

- `Parcel Pin brings atmosphere, discernment, and decision support into one experience, helping buyers choose well and helping developers sell with greater distinction.`

CTAs:

- primary: `Discuss a project`
- secondary: `Open the experience`

## Implementation Notes

- Build this as a **single page** at `/`
- Preserve the existing `/viewer` route and use it as the secondary proof CTA
- Keep the layout scrollable
- Do not add unnecessary pages
- Do not create a pricing section unless explicitly requested later
- Do not turn the page into a feature grid or product dashboard
- Use the Droneland ripped assets only if they help composition, not if they make the site feel agricultural or generic
- Avoid human/tractor imagery from the reference assets
- Abstract aerial imagery is acceptable as compositional texture if needed

---

## Design Constraints

- The first screen must feel like a premium poster, not a document
- The hero must be full-bleed or near full-bleed
- The page should rely on composition, typography, and image rhythm more than UI devices
- Keep sections spacious and calm
- Use one dominant idea per section
- Avoid unnecessary cards
- Avoid heavy gradients and flashy glow
- Keep accent colors minimal

---

## Acceptance Criteria

The landing page is successful if:

1. A visitor understands within a few seconds that Parcel Pin helps buyers **choose**, not merely browse.
2. The tone feels aligned with premium land in José Ignacio.
3. The page feels expensive, restrained, and intentional.
4. The experience does not resemble a generic SaaS homepage.
5. The page makes static brochures and simpler interactive parcel tools feel insufficient.
6. The developer value is clear, but the page remains emotionally buyer-led.
7. It works as a **single-page premium landing page**.
